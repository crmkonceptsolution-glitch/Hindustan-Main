What Is a HEPA Filter?

A HEPA (High Efficiency Particulate Air) filter is designed to capture 99.97% of airborne particles as small as 0.3 microns. These filters are widely used in environments where air purity is critical.

HEPA filters are commonly used for:

  • Cleanroom applications
  • Hospitals and healthcare facilities
  • Pharmaceutical manufacturing
  • Food processing units
  • HVAC systems

Industry Insight: HEPA filters are essential for maintaining contamination-free environments by effectively removing dust, allergens, bacteria, and microscopic particles from the air.

Types of HEPA Filters We Offer

1. Standard HEPA Filters

Designed for general air purification, these filters provide high efficiency and are suitable for HVAC systems and industrial use.

2. Mini Pleat HEPA Filters

Compact and highly efficient, mini pleat filters offer larger filtration area and improved airflow performance.

3. Deep Pleat HEPA Filters

These filters are ideal for applications requiring high dust holding capacity and long service life.
